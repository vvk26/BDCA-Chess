Belgaum District Chess Association (BDCA)

Belgaum District Chess Association (BDCA) was established on 11th January 1979 with the primary objective of promoting the game of chess in the region and officiating representation from Belgaum. The association was founded by a group of enthusiastic individuals, including AS Deshpande, Dadasaheb Deshpande, Prakash Kulkarni, Prakash Pawar, Lingsur brothers, Bhaskar Iyer, Deepak Deshpande, and Satyabodh Raichurkar. Soon after, AK Patankar, Ashok Pathak, and Ravi Naik joined the association, further expanding its reach.

Over the years, BDCA has organized numerous taluk, district, state, and national level chess events, including FIDE rated tournaments, which have provided opportunities for aspiring chess players to showcase their skills. The association has also conducted several training camps in the district to hone the skills of budding players.

BDCA's efforts have been fruitful, as some Belgaumites have gone on to become medalists at international levels, national champions, state champions, and have achieved several accolades. The association takes pride in the accomplishments of its members and continues to strive towards promoting the game of chess in the region.

BDCA's commitment to the game of chess and its contribution to the growth of the sport in the district has been exemplary. The association aims to continue its efforts to develop and promote the game of chess to a broader audience, and create opportunities for aspiring chess players to showcase their skills. BDCA aims to continue its legacy and is inclined to be instrumental in establishing Belagavi as a prominent center for chess in the state, so that it remains an important part of the region's cultural heritage.
